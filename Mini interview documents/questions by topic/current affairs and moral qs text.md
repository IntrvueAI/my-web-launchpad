# 11+ Interview Preparation Guide
### Top Independent Schools — Discussion & General Knowledge Questions

---

## PART 1: DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

*These questions have no right answer. Interviewers want to see curiosity, reasoning, and the ability to consider other points of view.*

---

### Current Affairs & The World

- Have you been following anything in the news recently? What did you make of it?
- Do you think social media does more good or more harm for people your age?
- Do you think richer countries have a responsibility to help poorer ones?
- If you had to pick one problem in the world to solve, what would it be and why?

---

### AI & Technology

- What do you think about AI — is it something to be excited about or worried about?
- If you used AI to help write an essay, would you consider that cheating?
- Do you think a computer could ever be truly creative — like write a poem that actually means something?
- Is there a job you think a robot could never do? Why?

---

### Climate & Environment

- Who do you think should be doing the most to tackle climate change — governments, businesses, or individuals?
- Is it fair that your generation has to deal with a problem it didn't really create?
- Is there something you'd genuinely be willing to give up to help the environment?

---

### Moral Dilemmas

- Say you found £50 on the pavement and there was no one around — what would you do?
- Can you think of a situation where lying might actually be the right thing to do?
- If you saw someone being bullied and stepping in felt risky, what would you do?
- If you could only save five strangers or one close friend, what would you do?

---

### Society, Fairness & Social Situations

- Do you think school uniforms are a good idea?
- Is it fair that some people are born into wealthy families and others aren't?
- Should 16-year-olds be allowed to vote? Why or why not?
- If you saw someone in your class being left out or picked on, what would you do?
- Have you ever had a falling out with a friend? How did you handle it, and would you do anything differently now?
- If your closest friend did something you thought was really wrong, would you say something to them?
- Say two of your friends had an argument and both came to you separately expecting you to take their side — how would you deal with that?

---

### Personal Curiosity & Interests Outside the Classroom

- Is there a book you've read that made you see something differently?
- What's something you've gone and looked into just because you wanted to know more about it?
- Has there been something you used to believe that you've since changed your mind on? What changed it?
- Is there a sport, hobby or activity you do outside of school that you're really passionate about?
- What's something you're good at that you've had to work hard to get better at?
- Have you ever taken on a leadership role — in a team, a club, or even just among friends? What did you learn from it?
- Is there a skill you've taught yourself, outside of school? How did you go about learning it?
- What do you do when you have completely free time and no one is telling you what to do?
- Has anything you've done outside school — a trip, a competition, a project — changed the way you think about something?

*Tip for candidates: For discussion questions, always give a reason for your answer. If the interviewer challenges you, don't immediately back down — think it through and explain your reasoning. It's fine to say "I hadn't thought of it that way, but I still think..." or "That's a good point, maybe I'd change my view on that because..."*
